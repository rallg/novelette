Terminology

The Copyright Page is the verso of the Title Page. It must be located in frontmatter. This page is "legalese" that descibes your book as an object of commerce, and library cataloging (if applicable).

The Copyright Page is unlike any other page in your book. It does not feature anything decorative. Certain text effects, such as curly quotes, are not used. Nothing is hyphenated. Lines are not justified. Text does not start at the top line of the page.

For your convenience, Novelette provides the copyrightpage environment. You do not have to use it, if you know how to do whatever needs to be done. But it is a very good idea to use this environment, since it does the thinking for you. See the example, lower on this page.

General Information

Do not use (c) to mean copyright. Either paste the actual symbol from a character map, or use \copyright code.

The copyright page must contain this information:

  • Title, subtitle (if any), author (possibly pseudonym). A widely-used format is The Title : The Subtitle / The Author. It may appear on more than one line.
  • Copyright statement. Minimal format: Copyright ©2024 Your Name. All Rights Reserved. Although it is not necessary to write both the word Copyright and the symbol, it is more emphatic.
  • If the author name is a pseudonym, you should use your real name as copyright holder, rather than the pseudonym. If someone violates your copyright, and the copyright holder is "Some Fakename" (pseudonym), then you will have to prove that "Some Fakename" is "Really You". But if the copyright holder is "Really You" then there is one less obstacle.
  • You may wish to write additional warnings and imprecations against copying. See other books in your genre, for examples. Under no circumstances use "copyleft" or anything less than full copyright. Many print-on-demand distributors refuse to carry books that have less than full copyright.
  • Use a disclaimer. Typically they say This is a work of fiction... and carry on from there. See other books in your genre for examples.
  • If you need to acknowledge use of a Trademark, quotation, or other permitted use, you may do it here. This is your responsibility.
  • Provide the name of the publisher, and publisher's location. The amount of detail may vary with jurisdiction. It may suffice to provide just the name and city, since other information can be obtained from the book distributor database. Or you may need to provide a full address or other information. In any case, the publisher is not the print-on-demand distributor or manufacturer. In most cases, the publisher is you.
  • Provide a means to contact the publisher (yourself). A web page URL is probably good enough. Some distributors provide an "author's page", but in most cases you cannot list that URL here, because (a) the distributor does not permit it, and (b) it is not a means to contact you.
  • Unless your book is printed in small quantity, for private use (not sold via distribution), then it needs an ISBN. Show the ISBN in its standard format. Do not show the barcode. Depending on jurisdiction, you may be able to get a government-issued ISBN upon request, or the distributor may provide an ISBN for free or small charge. Beware of procuring an ISBN from someone else, because that number will be registered to whomever supplied it to you, rather than to the distributor or yourself.
  • If your book has library catalog data (unlikely), provide it in the standard format. Nowadays, popular fiction rarely has catalog data, because libraries list popular fiction by genre (crime, romance, sci-fi, etc.) and author surname, not by catalog number. You may see "Publisher's Cataloging-in-Publication Data available upon request." The publisher may not really have it, and nobody may request it.
  • Many jurisdictions require that the name and location of printer be shown, in a particular location. This is known as Impressum. But with print-on-demand, you do not know where any particular copy will be printed, or who will print it. The required information will probably be added by the printer at time of printing, on the final verso page. If in doubt, ask the distributor.

Sample Code for Copyright Page

The above sounds like a lot of information, but it is not. For many books, the copyright page will look like this:

\begin{copyrightpage}
My Wonderful Story : An Awesome Tale / Tony Baloney
 
Copyright ©2023 Tommy Salami. All Rights Reserved.
 
This is a work of fiction. All characters and events are from the author's imagination. Locations are used fictionally.
 
Published by the copyright holder, CityName NationName.
Online http://www.example.com/baloneytales
 
ISBN 0-00000-0000-00-0
\end{copyrightpage}
