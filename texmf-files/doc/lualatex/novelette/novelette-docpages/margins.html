Interior Page Margins

ON THIS PAGE: Diagram of Margins   \margins

Terminology

When you open a book, the page at left is verso, with an even number. The page at right is recto, with an odd number. In many cases, no number is printed, although it exists in the PDF.

In Novelette, the four margins are boundaries of a rectangle. This rectangle is the printable area. All content (including header, footer, main text, and images) must fit within the printable area.

Some word processors do not define "margins" the same way. Instead, they place the header within the area of the top margin, and place the footer within the area of the bottom margin. If the manufacturer suggests margins, be sure that the values account for the way Novelette does it, not the way a word processor does it.

\margins{t=T, o=O, b=B, i=I}

The four margins are top, outside, bottom, inside (spine). Values for T, O, B, and I must have units of in (inches) or mm (millimeters). Example:

\margins{t=.5in, o=.5in, b=.5in, i=.625in}

If you do not set margins, Novelette will use default values. If you set any margin, then you must set all four of them.

Inside margin is normally wider than outside margin, because inside margin includes the glue binding area. Typical extra width is 3mm or 0.125in.

Novelette does not allow page bleed, which involves printed content (usually images) reaching the page edge. All margins must have a value greater then zero.

Manufacturers require minimum margins. Novelette defaults are 13mm top, outside, and bottom; 16.2mm inside. This appears to meet the requirements of known American and European manufacturers. Some allow smaller values.

Artistic effects, such as an extra-wide outside or bottom margin, may be rejected by the manufacturer. This is because the humans who handle the pages have never seen your book before, and may think that the printer was mis-fed, or the pages mis-glued.
