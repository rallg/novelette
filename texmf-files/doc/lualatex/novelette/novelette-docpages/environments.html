Introduction

An environment starts with \begin{something} and ends with \end{something}. The \begin tells the compiler to use certain settings, which will be applied until \end.

Use only the environments described on this page. LaTeX has many built-in environments, most of which are only relevant to math or academic works. Novelette disallows them. If you attempt to use a disallowed environment, then an error may result.

top

Environment: document

\begin{document} ... \end{document}

This environment is required. Its contents are the "document body" which will print.

  • The Preamble precedes \begin{document}.
  • Anything written after \end{document} is ignored. You may write notes to yourself there, if you wish.

Environment: fullpage

\begin{fullpage} ... \end{fullpage}

The fullpage environment begins a new page. When it ends, the following material begin on a new page.

  • Do not confuse this environment with a full-page floated image.
  • The fullpage is intended for just one page. However, if there is too much inside, it continues to a second page, also fullpage. An alert is issued.
  • A fullpage does not print header or footer. You may locally over-ride using \thispagestyle.
  • You may use ordinary text, \image, \icon, and the styling commands \name \subname \format \smalltext inside fullpage.
  • You may not use \footnote. But you may use \vfill to spread whatever is above it, from whatever is below it.
  • You may nest an upperpage environment at the top of fullpage.
  • You may nest the center and blockindent environments within fullpage.
  • If you place an \image in fullpage, it cannot float, but must appear where you write the command.

Environment: legal

\begin{legal} ... \end{legal}

Like fullpage, the legal environment begins and ends with a new page, and does not print header or footer. But unlike fullpage, the legal environment restricts what you can do inside it.

  • The legal environment is designed for use as the copyright page.
  • You do not need to use this environment, if you prefer to style the copyright page yourself. But legal is recommended, because it pre-styles its content, and thus involves less coding on your part.
  • You cannot nest legal within fullpage, because legal already has its own fullpage wrapper.
  • Within legal :
    • Text is horizontally and vertically centered.
    • Hyphenation is disabled.
    • Numbers are lining, not oldstyle.
    • No indentation.
    • Paragraphs are separated by a blank line.
    • You may not use \image, but you may use \icon.
    • Commands \name \subname \format are disabled.
    • You may not nest another environment.

More information is lower on this page.

Environment: upperpage

\begin{upperpage} ... \end{upperpage}

The upperpage environment is used at the start of new chapters. This may include some non-chapter material that is styled like the first page of a chapter.

See the chapters page for more information.

Environment: blockindent

\begin{blockindent}[options] ... \end{blockindent}

This environment indents all of its content at left and/or right, relative to main text. It is useful for poetry (verse), lengthy quotations, and the like. It is also useful for narrowing the textwidth, so that the included text occupies several part-width lines, instead of one or two full-width lines.

  • The block may contain more than one paragraph.
  • If no option is provided, the block is indented 1 indent at left, none at right.
  • If the option is an integer, such as [3], then the block is indented that many indents at left, none at right.
  • If the option is two comma-separated integers, such as [3,2], then they are applied at left and right, in that order.
  • Although you may use decimal numbers instead of integers, this is discouraged, because it may look wrong. But if you wish to do something such as set a block at exactly half the text width, with the block centered, [.25,.25] does that.
  • The width of the block must be at least 0.4 of the full textwidth.
  • Within the block, paragraph indent is set to zero. If that is not what you need, use \forceindent at the beginning of each paragraph within the block.
  • If the blockindent sits within a fullpage or upperpage environment, then style commands \name \subname \format \smalltext may be placed within the blockindent. But if the blockindent does not sit within those environments, they style commands may not be used.
  • You may not place a blockindent within any of the above style commands.
  • You may not use \image within blockindent. You may use \icon.
  • You may use the center environment inside blockindent, but not the other way around: You cannot use blockindent within center.

Environment: center

\begin{center} ... \end{center}

Everything within this environment is centered.

  • The contents may contain more than one paragraph.
  • When center sits within fullpage or \upperpage, you may use style commands.
  • If a style command, or image, uses [align=setting], the setting will be ignored.
  • If you do not wish the text to reach the full textwidth, you may place center inside blockindent.
  • Unlike other LaTeX document classes, Novelette does not insert additional line space above or below center.
  • You may not place blockindent inside center.
  • You may place center between paragraphs of main text.

top

No Others!

LaTeX provides many other built-in environments. However, you cannot use them in Novelette. Among them are list, table, flushright, equation, etc.

Novelette attempts to intercept requests for the prohibited environments. An error or alert is issued. But this is not foolproof.

You have been warned!

Details

About Copyright Page

A very minimal copyright page might look like this:

\begin{legal}
\vfil
 My Wonderful Story :\\
An Awesome Tale\\
/ Tony Baloney
 
Copyright ©2024 Tommy Salami. All Rights Reserved.
 
This is a work of fiction. All characters and events are from the author's imagination. Locations are used fictionally.
 
Published by the copyright holder, CityName NationName.
Online http://www.example.com/baloneytales
 
ISBN 0-00000-0000-00-0
\vfil
\end{legal}

Whether you use the legal environment, or create your own design within fullpage, it must be the page (verso) that immediately follows the book title page.

The copyright page must contain this information:

  • Title, subtitle (if any), author (possibly pseudonym). A widely-used format is The Title : The Subtitle / The Author. It may appear on more than one line.
  • Copyright statement. Minimal format: Copyright ©2024 Your Name. All Rights Reserved. Some jurisdictions use lengthier statements. Although it is not necessary to write both the word Copyright and the symbol, it is more emphatic. Do not write (c). Either paste the actual copyright symbol from a character map, or use \copyright.
  • If the author name is a pseudonym, you should use your real name as copyright holder, rather than the pseudonym. Reason: If someone violates your copyright, and the copyright holder is "Some Fakename" (pseudonym), then you will have to prove that "Some Fakename" is "Really You". But if the copyright holder is "Really You" then there is one less obstacle.
  • Under no circumstances use "copyleft" or anything less than full copyright. Many print-on-demand distributors refuse to carry books that have less than full copyright.
  • Use a disclaimer. Typically they say This is a work of fiction... and carry on from there. See other books in your genre for examples.
  • If you need to acknowledge use of a Trademark, quotation, or other permitted use, you may do it here. This is your responsibility.
  • Provide the name of the publisher, and publisher's location. In the case of self-published works, the publisher is you, not the distributor or manufacturer. Most print-on-demand services will not allow you to mention them.
  • For the publisher's location, The amount of required detail may vary with jurisdiction. It may suffice to provide just your name and city/nation, since other information can be obtained from the book distributor database.
  • Provide a means to contact the publisher (yourself). Commercial publishers provide all of street addrress, telephone number, and web page URL. But for a self-publisher without a business address, that much information (your home address and phone) might be too much. Perhaps your own web page URL is good enough. Some distributors provide an "author's page", but in most cases you cannot list that URL here, because (a) the distributor does not permit it, and (b) it is not a means to contact you, and (c) If you change the distributor, then the URL is no longer valid.
  • Unless your book is printed in small quantity, for private use (not sold via distribution), then it needs an ISBN. Show the ISBN in its standard format. Do not show the barcode. Depending on jurisdiction, you may be able to get a government-issued ISBN upon request, or the distributor may provide an ISBN for free or small charge. Beware of procuring an ISBN from someone else, because that number will be registered to whomever supplied it to you, rather than to the distributor or yourself.
  • If your book has library catalog data (unlikely), provide it in the standard format. Nowadays, popular fiction rarely has catalog data, because libraries list popular fiction by genre (crime, romance, sci-fi, etc.) and author surname, not by catalog number. You may see "Publisher's Cataloging-in-Publication Data available upon request." The publisher may not really have it, and nobody may request it.
  • Many jurisdictions require that the name and location of printer be shown, in a particular location. This is known as Impressum. But with print-on-demand, you do not know where any particular copy will be printed, or who will print it. The required information will probably be added by the printer at time of printing, on the final verso page. If in doubt, ask the distributor.
