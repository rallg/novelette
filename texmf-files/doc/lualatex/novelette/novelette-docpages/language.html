Language

In order to do one thing well, instead of many things, Novelette is limited to the English language.

You may write the occasional word or phrase in another language. If you do, it will be hyphenated according to English hyphenation rules.

The language only sets hyphenation, according to the slight spelling differences. It does not check spelling or grammar, does not format dat, time, or money, or anything else.

\english{us or uk}

The command for setting language is \english, and there are only two choices: us or uk. If red is a color, your choice is us. If red is a colour, your choice is uk.

If you do not set this, then an older, generic hyphenation pattern will be used.
