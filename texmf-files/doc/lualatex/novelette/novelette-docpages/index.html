Things That Are Not Part of Novelette

Unavailable: table of contents

There is no provision for a table of contents, whether automatic or manual. Novelette was created for the kind of book that does not use it.

If you absolutely must have a table of contents, then design it yourself, and put it on page v instead of anything else that might go there. It must be limited to one page. In any event, the page numbers will not be populated automatically.

Unavailable: vector drawing

Novelette supports raster graphics only. No vector drawings from pgf, TiKz, svg, eps, included pdf, or any other means.

If desired, you can create vector drawings in a different document class, then rasterize them to monochrome black/white at 600 pixels/inch, using a graphis program. They must be trimmed to final size. Novelette does not apply transforms to graphics objects.

Why this limitation? Print-on-demand services sometimes reject files with vector graphics. Even a simple drawing, which conforms to coding format, may be difficult to interpret correctly. It may look OK with your software, but have a different meaning to the printer software. Or, it may take a long time to interpret. This is not specific to TeX.

Unavailable: drop cap

In some books, new chapters begin with an enlarged letter (or image) that occupies more than one line of text. The main text flows around it.

Although TeX can do this, Novelette does not. It is rarely seen in popular fiction, and may cause other layout issues. Instead, Novelette offers the \init{letter}{some text} command, which creates a style that is widely used.

Unavailable: drop folio

When page layout has not footer, the page numbers appear in header. Thus, on pages without printed header (such as first page of a chapter), no page number appears.

In professional works, citations require page numbers. To assist this, pages without header have the page number (term: folio) "dropped" into a one-page temporary footer. This is known as "drop folio."

Novelette does not have drop folio. It is not needed for popular fiction, and although it could be programmed, it could interfere with other page layout.

Unavailable: Split Page Spread

Some books have split page spreads. The verso of page N, and recto of page N+!, face each other when the book is opened there. The right side of N continues across the binding, to the left side of N+1.

Novelette does not, and cannot, do this. Print-on-demand does not allow it. Of course, you could design the pages so that they play well together, even though there is a large gap on both pages at the binding.

In all cases, the title page must be a single page. You cannot write part of the title on N, another part at N+1.
