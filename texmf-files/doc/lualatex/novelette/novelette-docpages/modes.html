What Are Modes?

Novelette has several modes. All modes have the same typesetting, so paragraphs and page breaks are the same. But other information may be added to, or removed from the PDF, to help you edit your work.

top

Draft Mode

\mode{draft}

This is the default. In draft mode, over-long lines (cannot fit in the width of the textblock) will show a black bar at the end, known as the overflow rule. Images and icons will display, but they will not be checked for conformance with PDF/X requirements. The PDF will not contain metadata identifying it as PDF/X. The PDF title will have the word DRAFT before the actual title, so it is not confused with the final PDF.

Since draft mode usually compiles faster than any other mode, you should use draft mode while you are writing and editing your work.

top

Shade Mode

\mode{shade} or \mode{shade*}

Shade mode is also draft mode. In shade mode, the margins and some other areas are shaded gray. This is useful for visualizing layout. It is illustrated on another page.

With the asterisk, shade* adds light gray vertical lines in the textblock, spaced at integer number of paragraph indents from the side margins. Maximum 9 lines per side. This is useful for seeing how blockindent aligns to the page.

top

Fill Mode

\mode{fill}

Fill mode is also draft/shade mode. In fill mode, a few pages of nonsense text are printed, in the style of a mainmatter chapter. You do not need to provide any text in the document body. But if you do, it prints after the nonsense has finished.

top

Typo Mode

\mode{typo} or \mode{typo*}

Typo mode is also draft mode. In typo mode, a variety of typographic flaws are hightlighted. This is useful for improving the quality of the text. It is described in detail on another page.

top

Final Mode

\mode{final}

Final mode is required when you are ready to submit your PDF. In final mode, some additional checks are performed. If any check fails, the PDF will be demoted to draft mode. If all checks pass, then the PDF will be identified as PDF/X-1a:2001 conformant, according to the \intent setting.
