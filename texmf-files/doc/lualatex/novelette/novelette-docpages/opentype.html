OpenType Features

This page is under development.

OpenType features are font-specific. Some features, such as onum, (oldstyle numerals) have a standard meaning, but not every font will have them. Other standard features, such as sups (superscripts) may include only numerals in one font, but more than just numerals in another font. Yet other features, such as ss01, have no standard meaning; two fonts may have that feature, but its effect may be very different.

The only way to know, is to test the font.

In Novelette, OpenType features are selected by their four-character codes. You may locally add features, or locally remove features that were selected in Preamble. Removal is by negation: -liga removes common ligatures.

Although Novelette relies on fontspec technology, your use of fontspec commands is very limited. The reason is that fontspec allows many variations that would work in a digital document, but fail when commercially printed to paper. Novelette tries very hard to prevent you from using things that may cause issues.

In general, any fontspec feature that uses the equal sign (=) is banned. Example: You cannot use Letters=SmallCaps, but you may request lowercase to small caps using smcp as a feature.

The one exception is the fontspec Path feature. You probably will never need it, but Novelette allows it.
