Terminology

The printer is a machine that prints, or whoever operates the machine. The manufacturer creates a finished object (your book) with printed pages, glued together at spine, with cover. A "print-on-demand" (POD) service is actually manufacture-on-demand, with distribution from one or more factories to destinations.

A page is one side of a leaf. Thus, every book necessarily has an even number of pages. Manufacturers count every one of them, no matter how they are numbered (or not), even blanks. The finished book may contain extra pages at the end, added by the manufacturer as part of workflow. The final page, which faces the rear cover, is always blank (although the manufacturer may add something there).

The trim size is the width and height of the book interior pages. With most softcover technology, that is also the size of the finished book. But in the case of library hardcover books, trim size refers to the pages, and the cover is slightly larger.

The most common method of softcover POD is named "Perfect Print"â„¢. When the pages are glued together, and the cover is attached, all of it is slightly over-size. Then the entire assembly is trimmed to final size, This is why such books have all pages and cover so precisely aligned.

\trimsize{w=width,h=height}

You must set trimsize. There is no default, because it it the most important setting.

Width and height must be specified with units of in (inches) or mm (millimeters). You must provide actual values. Novelette does not offer you a choice of size names.

Here a some popular (and suggested) choices:

\trimsize{w=5.5in,h=8.5in} % U.S.A.

\trimsize{w=135mm,h=210mm} % British B-Format.

Choose only a standard size, offered by the manufacturer, with off-white paper. Off-white paper may be known as "creme" or "cream".
