Special Pages

Introduction

Most pages in frontmatter have a special style, that distinguishes them from other pages: half-title, frontispiece, title, copyright, dedication, contents, epigraph, acknowledgements/note.

Methods for styling these pages are described here, with examples. Chapter openings are descibed on another page.

Half-Title Page

Usually, the half-title page is the first page of frontmatter. It is recto, page i (roman numeral), without header/footer (and thus no printed page number). It faces the inside of the front cover.

Historically, the book interior pages were printed by a business that did nothing but print, then later bound into a book by another business that did nothing but bookbinding. The half-title was a dust cover for the real title page. Although that is obsolete, the practice continues.

The half-title page must contain nothing but the book title. No subtitle, no author. It looks best when the title uses the same style (text or image) as on the title page, but at size between ordinary text and the title page.

The half-title must sit within a fullpage environment. Here is an example, where the title has been written on several lines, for pleasing visual design:

\begin{fullpage}
\vfil
\format[scale=1.6,font=light]{Excerpts}
\format[scale=1.3,font=light]{from}
\format[scale=1.6,font=light]{Mark Twain}
\vfil\vfil
\end{fullpage}

In the above example, the default \format alignment is horizontally centered. By using only one \vfil at top, but two of them at bottom, the text is placed higher than mid-page. The font=light assumes that the book title (on the title page) also uses that font.

In some books, the half-title is repeated as the last recto page in frontmatter. You may do that, if you wish.

\dedication{text}

Contrary to popular belief, a book does not need a dedication. But if you wish to write one, it must be placed by itself, on the page that immediately follows the copyright page.

The fullpage environment will isolate the dedication, and ensure that no header or footer appears there.

Novelette provides the \dedication command. Its text will be centered horizontally, in Italics, at slightly larger than normal size. This is the most common style used in books. But you may use other methods to style it yourself. If the dedication is more than a few words, use \\ or \br to break the text into multiple short lines.

Although it is very unusual, you may use \image and \icon on the dedication page. Avoid using other styled text.

To position the dedication mid-page, use \vfil above and below it. To raise it slightly above mid-page, write one or more \null commands.

Example dedication:

\begin{fullpage}
\vfil
\dedication{For Homies Everywhere}
\null\null
\vfil
\end{fullpage}

Table of Contents

Unlike other LaTeX document classes, Novelette has no provision for a table of contents. You may design one yourself, but its chapter numbers and page numbers are not automated: You must manually write them, after the rest of the book has been typeset (so you know the numbers).

Unless your book is a collection of short stories, a table of contents is discouraged.

If you use a table of contents, it is a recto page, which may flow to a following verso. You may place the table immediately after the copyright page, if you wish; but if there is a dedication, the table must come after the dedication.

Novelette provides commands \entry and \linespread to help you. Each entry is horizontally centered, with chapter and page separated by three spaced dots. If there are few entries, the linespread can move them apart. Or if the table flows to a second page with only a line or two, the linespread can move more lines there.

Use \format to style the table title. Trick: Instead of saying "Table of Contents" in your language, use the title of the book.

Example table of contents:

\begin{fullpage}
\null\null\null
\linespread{1.2}
\format[scale=1.7]{The Wonk, and Other Stories}
\null
\entry{Bread Pudding}{1}
\entry{Homie Escapade}{24}
\entry{Off the Rails}{53}
\entry{The Wonk}{127}
\end{fullpage}
